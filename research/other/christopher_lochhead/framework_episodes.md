# Christopher Lochhead — Framework Episode Notes

Supplementary to `../../youtube-transcripts/christopher_lochhead/`. These are evergreen
"Category Design" episodes with full writeups on lochhead.com (richer than the YouTube
descriptions), selected for the frameworks they teach rather than as raw transcripts.

## LOM 212: "What Most People Don't Know About Politics: How Big Change Actually Happens"
- **Date:** 2026-05-27
- **URL:** https://lochhead.com/lom-what-most-people-dont-know-about-politics-2026/

**Framework:**
Category design lens applied to elections — "the person who names the problem gets to
define the solution." Candidates who frame the problem control the conversation; those
defending records play on someone else's field. Examples: OpenAI (generative AI vs.
better search), Spanx (shapewear), plus current campaign case studies.

**Why it's useful:**
Cleanest recent articulation of problem-framing as positioning — directly portable to
SaaS category narratives.

## LOM 215: "The Asset AI Can't Steal" (with Gina Bianchini)
- **Date:** 2026-06-25
- **URL:** https://lochhead.com/lom-gina-bianchini-2026/

**Framework:**
Four capitals — financial, reputation, intellectual, relationship — and "people magic."
As AI commoditizes knowledge/execution, community and relationships become the defensible
moat.

**Why it's useful:**
Frames community as a strategic asset in an AI-saturated content landscape.

## LOM 210: "Is Agentic AI the End of SaaS as We Know It?" (DisrupTV)
- **Date:** ~2025-08
- **URL:** https://lochhead.com/lom-disruptv-2025/

**Framework:**
"The Existing Market Trap" — companies fail by benchmarking against old categories; ask
"what new problem are we solving and what category defines it?" Also: AI as co-founder
(exponential) vs. copilot (incremental); "the most powerful thing you can ship is a new
belief."

**Why it's useful:**
Category design applied specifically to B2B SaaS + AI positioning.

## LOM 211: "The Enduring Power of Positioning" (with Laura Ries, Part 1)
- **Date:** ~2025-08
- **URL:** https://lochhead.com/lom-laura-ries-pt1-2025/

**Framework:**
Attention vs. ownable position (American Eagle/Sydney Sweeney vs. Calvin Klein/Brooke
Shields); if you're not the category king, establish a well-defined opposite position with
a strategic enemy. Dude Wipes / Liquid Death as category-design cases.

**Why it's useful:**
Positioning-vs-virality argument with memorable case pairs — useful against
"engagement-bait" content strategies.
